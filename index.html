Radha Raman Ji

🪷

The Divine Form

Shree Radha Raman Ji is one of the most sacred and revered deities of Vrindavan — the self-manifested form of Bhagwan Shri Krishna that emerged miraculously from a Shaligram Shila in 1542 CE, as revealed to the great Vaishnava saint Gopal Bhatt Goswami.

Unlike most temple deities, Radha Raman Ji is unique in that no separate Radha idol is present — instead, a golden crown (mukut) beside the Lord represents Shri Radha Rani, embodying their eternal union and divine love.

The temple is one of the seven principal temples of Vrindavan established by the Six Goswamis, the foremost disciples of Shri Chaitanya Mahaprabhu. The flame lit in 1542 has never been extinguished.
